The Scene milestones

In the early days of modern computing, the terms microcomputer and personal computer were interchangeable. This site defines the PC as the Intel-compatible x86 architecture known initially as the IBM PC but evolved to MS-DOS and Windows. All other personal computer platforms get referenced as microcomputers.

These milestones are for the PC underground and cracking scenes. They are not definitive but are based on the digital artifacts collected. The more notable communities on other microcomputer platforms are seldom mentioned here, including the famed Apple II, Commodore 64, and Commodore Amiga communities, which were often poorly imitated by the underground communities on the PC.
