UCF would also venture into the PC gaming Scene, for awhile [releasing](/f/af22407) full titles including CD-RIPs, but later, mostly releasing self-contained cracks, updates, game trainers and cheats. As well as using the UCF label to release their own tools and utilities, both for the Scene and more general online users.

Years later, the group would [claim](/f/a9272de) they were the first cracking group, _You wanna be a part of the FIRST cracking-group?_ This suggestion is rubbish, as there were cracking groups on the Commodore 64 and Apple in the 1980s. However, they were probably one of the first Scene groups to focus on creating and distributing cracks and game trainers, rather than releasing cracked, copy-protected software.
